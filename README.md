
GreenRoots Ventures is a climate-focused social enterprise based in Lilongwe, Malawi, that transforms agricultural waste into valuable resources such as biochar, organic fertilizer, and clean cooking briquettes. The initiative addresses key challenges faced by rural communities, including deforestation, declining soil fertility, and limited income opportunities for youth and women.
By integrating maize farming, livestock production, and waste recycling, GreenRoots creates a circular system where agricultural residues like maize cobs, rice husks, and groundnut shells are converted into sustainable products. These products help farmers improve soil health and increase crop yields, while briquettes provide households with a cleaner and more affordable alternative to charcoal.
The enterprise works directly with local communities, engaging youth and women in production and distribution activities to create income opportunities and build local capacity. Through this approach, GreenRoots promotes climate resilience, reduces environmental impact, and supports sustainable livelihoods.

The Problem
Over 90% of households in Malawi rely on charcoal and firewood
This drives deforestation and environmental degradation
Farmers face poor soil fertility and high fertilizer costs
Agricultural waste is often burned or discarded
💡 Our Solution
We use a circular farming approach to turn waste into value:
♻️ Convert agricultural waste into biochar, briquettes, and organic fertilizer
🌱 Improve soil fertility and increase crop yields
🔥 Provide clean and affordable cooking fuel
👩🏽‍🌾 Create income opportunities for youth and women
🔄 How It Works
Agricultural Waste → Processing → Biochar / Briquettes / Fertilizer → Farmers & Households
📊 Impact
Reduces deforestation and waste burning
Improves soil health and food production
Creates jobs for youth and women
Promotes climate resilience in rural communities
🎯 Vision
To build a scalable, community-driven system that transforms waste into opportunity, strengthens food security, and supports sustainable livelihoods across Malawi and beyond.
